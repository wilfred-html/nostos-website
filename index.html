αʹ Chapter one  ·  the name

A return, not an arrival.

Nostos is an old Greek word for the ache of coming home — the longing that pulls a traveller back across water, back to the kitchen they grew up in, back to the table where everyone is already sitting down. We named the restaurant for that feeling because it's the one we wanted you to leave with.

The food is not reinvented. It is remembered. Lamb that has cooked since the morning. Tomatoes dressed only with what they need. Bread torn, not sliced. A pour of something cold, because you brought it yourself and we kept the glass cold for you.

Hout Bay holds a particular kind of light in the late afternoon — low, salted, a little tired from the day. That is the hour we built the room for. Come before then, or come right at it, but come hungry either way.

Every plate, every flavour, has a purpose and a story.

What was wrong before

The old version looked assembled, not designed.

It had the right intention, but not the right structure. It read more like a concept note with an embedded booking widget than a finished restaurant website.

A proper site needs hierarchy, visual pacing, distinct section roles, and a layout system that guides the eye naturally. That’s what this rebuild is solving first.

Design principle

Proportion, rhythm, and restraint.

I rebuilt the page around stronger spacing ratios, cleaner content grouping, and a more balanced primary layout. The hero now uses a wider-to-narrower composition closer to a golden-ratio split, which gives the page more natural visual tension.

That makes it feel calmer, more premium, and less like stacked blocks fighting each other.

Brand foundation
“Our food is inspired by memory: every plate, every flavour, has a purpose and a story.”

This is still the core truth. The site should feel like that line belongs to a serious restaurant with identity — not just copy pasted onto a landing page.

Story direction

A homecoming, translated into digital form.

Nostos is strongest when it leans into emotional clarity: Greek roots, warm hospitality, and the feeling of returning somewhere meaningful. That gives us a brand world to design from.

The website should support that with editorial typography, breathing room, and imagery-led atmosphere instead of over-decorated restaurant clichés.

Experience architecture

What a proper Nostos site should communicate

A restaurant website is not just “about + booking.” It needs to answer four things quickly: what kind of place this is, why it matters, what it feels like to visit, and how to book without friction.

01

Identity first

Greek, story-led, and rooted in memory. The visual language should make that obvious before anyone reads deep body copy.

02

Atmosphere second

The layout should create appetite and trust through tone, rhythm, and space — not just blocks of information.

03

Conversion without ugliness

Bookings matter, but they should be integrated into a premium brand experience, not dominate it like a cheap widget page.

Visual direction for the next iteration

Replace the temporary logo-based visuals with real food, interior, table, and exterior photography. That one change will dramatically increase credibility.

Signals to keep

What already feels right

Warm Mediterranean palette Olive, sand, terracotta, and soft paper tones support the Greek/coastal identity well.
Serif-led typography Cormorant gives the brand elegance and emotion where a generic sans-first system would flatten it.
Direct booking integration Dineplan is already the live conversion path, so it should stay embedded and easy to reach.

What still needs work

The next version should go much further.

You were right to call this out. To make Nostos feel like a proper website, the next pass should add real imagery, richer menu storytelling, a proper location/contact block, and stronger social proof.

But structurally, this version is now heading in the right direction: clearer hierarchy, better sectioning, improved visual rhythm, and less “AI page” energy.

Next action
